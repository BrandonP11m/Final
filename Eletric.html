Electric Cars

“EV” is the catch-all term for an electric vehicle. Fully electric cars get all of their power from motors that use batteries charged with electricity. Another type, the fuel cell electric vehicle, or FCEV, runs on compressed hydrogen. The limited availability of hydrogen fuel cell cars is in California due to limited infrastructure available to the public outside of that state..

What to Know About All-Electric Cars

Owning an EV has many benefits, whether it’s a fully electric model or a plug-in hybrid. For starters, all EVs have lower overall fuel costs. Fully electric vehicles often have lower maintenance costs because they have fewer components than gas-powered cars. Battery-powered electric cars don’t require oil changes or tune-ups, typically only rotating their specialized tires and wiper replacement. Fully electric cars never need to stop at a gas station for fuel before running errands around town because their batteries can be charged at home when not in use..
